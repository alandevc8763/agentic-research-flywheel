# 🛡️ Epistemic Critique Framework: Automated Verification for the Research Flywheel

## 1. Theoretical Foundation
The Epistemic Critique Framework ($\text{ECF}$) is designed to maximize the Signal-to-Noise Ratio ($\text{SNR}$) of the Second Brain by implementing a rigorous, adversarial verification layer between the **Distillation Engine** and the **Sync Manager**.

Based on the *Accuracy-Correction Paradox*, we recognize that high-capability models often produce "deep errors" that resist intrinsic self-correction. Therefore, $\text{ECF}$ shifts from *self-reflection* to *externalized, multi-perspective debate*.

## 2. The Verification Pipeline ($\text{VP}$)
The verification process is decomposed into three discrete stages to avoid the cognitive collapse typical of single-pass correction:

$$\text{Artifact} \xrightarrow{\text{Detection}} \text{Error Map} \xrightarrow{\text{Localization}} \text{Correction Logic} \xrightarrow{\text{Consensus}} \text{Verified Artifact}$$

### 2.1 Stage I: Adversarial Detection (The Panel)
Instead of a single critic, $\text{ECF}$ employs a **Competitive Debate Panel** embodying distinct epistemic roles:
- **The Pedant ($\mathcal{P}$)**: Focuses on formal correctness, citation accuracy, and LaTeX precision.
- **The Skeptic ($\mathcal{S}$)**: Searches for logical leaps, unfounded assertions, and "hallucinated" correlations.
- **The Architect ($\mathcal{A}$)**: Evaluates structural coherence and alignment with the `NORTH_STAR` vision.

### 2.2 Stage II: Epistemic Blinding ($\mathcal{B}$)
To eliminate parametric bias (prior contamination), critical claims are passed through an **Epistemic Blinding** layer:
1. Entity identifiers (e.g., "DeepSeek-R1") are replaced with anonymous tokens (e.g., $\text{Entity}_{\alpha}$).
2. The panel evaluates the *logic* of the claim without knowing the *subject*.
3. Results are compared against an unblinded control to detect "Brand Bias".

### 2.3 Stage III: Peer Reliability Estimation ($\text{PRE}$)
To prevent sycophancy (blind conformity), each agent's critique is weighted by a **Trust Score** $\tau$:
$$\tau_{t+1} = \tau_t + \eta (\mathbb{1}_{\text{verified}} - \tau_t)$$
Where $\eta$ is the learning rate and $\mathbb{1}_{\text{verified}}$ is a binary indicator of whether the critique correctly identified a later-verified error.

## 3. Implementation Protocol
For every `KnowledgeArtifact` generated by the Distiller:
1. **Initialize Debate**: Generate three independent critiques ($\mathcal{P}, \mathcal{S}, \mathcal{A}$).
2. **Blinded Audit**: Perform $\mathcal{B}$ on top-priority claims.
3. **Synthesize Corrections**: Use a `ConsensusAgent` to merge valid critiques into a final set of edits.
4. **Final Gate**: The artifact is synced only if $\text{SNR}_{\text{final}} > \text{Threshold}_{\text{Elite}}$.

## 4. Success Metrics ($\text{KPIs}$)
- **Detection Recall**: $\frac{\text{Errors Detected}}{\text{Total Errors in Dataset}}$.
- **Sycophancy Rate**: Percentage of critiques that agree with the Distiller despite the presence of an error.
- **Blinding Delta**: $\Delta = |\text{Score}_{\text{blinded}} - \text{Score}_{\text{unblinded}}|$.
